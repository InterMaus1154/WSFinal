Hydroelectric

Hydroelectric energy, also called hydroelectric power or hydroelectricity, is a form of energy that harnesses the power of water in motion - such as water flowing over a waterfall to generate electricity. People have used this force for millennia.

Wind Turbines

A wind turbine is a device that converts the kinetic energy of wind into electrical energy. Hundreds of thousands of large turbines, in installations known as wind farms, now generate over 650 gigawatts of power, with 60 GW added each year.

Solar Photovoltaics

Solar energy is energy from the sun, and it's the most abundant energy resource on Earth. It can be converted from radiant energy from the sun to electrical or thermal energy in order to be used for various purposes including powering homes, vehicles and other devices.
